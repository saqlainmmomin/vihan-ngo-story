Every life on the street
deserves a second chance.

The story of Vihan and his family — and the dogs, puppies and cattle they love back to life.

Who we are

A home where the helpless are never turned away

It started the way most rescues do — one trembling puppy that nobody else would stop for. Today, Vihan and his family run a small, self-funded rescue out of their home. There's no big building and no salary. Just a family that refuses to walk past suffering — and the rescued ones who've become part of the family.

“We can't save every animal on the street. But the one in front of us, today — that one, we can.”
The everyday belief behind the work
01 — The rescue

It begins with one small life

Most arrive frightened, hungry and alone — pulled from gutters, roadsides and market corners. Some are only days old, with eyes barely open. The first hours are about warmth, a borrowed sweater, food, and the simple discovery that a human hand can be gentle.

02 — The healing

Late nights and never giving up

Injured dogs get IV drips, bandages, recovery cones and round-the-clock attention — often through the night. Every drip, every dressing, every small meal is a step back toward a life worth wagging for.

03 — Beyond dogs

Care for the cattle, too

Cows and bullocks that collapse on the road — too weak or injured to rise — are brought in, slung upright, fed and treated for as long as it takes. It's heavy, demanding work few are willing to take on. Here, no animal is too big or too broken to help.

04 — The family that forms

Where new beginnings happen

A mother and her litter, safe under a roof. Recovering dogs napping in the sun. Pups who arrived terrified now playing, trusting, becoming dogs again. This is what all the hard nights are for.
